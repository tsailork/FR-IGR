1. The Core Implementation Rules for HLLC & AUSM

When transitioning from a simple Rusanov (Lax-Friedrichs) flux to multi-wave solvers like HLLC or flux-splitting schemes like AUSM+, the introduction of Phantom Pressure Regularization requires strict separation of concerns between two distinct pressure fields:

$$\text{Acoustic Boundary Estimation (Wave Speeds): } \bar{P} = \max(P_{reg}, P_{phys})$$ $$\text{Flux & Enthalpy Transformations (Conservation): } P_{reg} = P_{phys} + \theta (P_{phys} - P_{phan})$$

Rule 1: The safe pressure \(\bar{P}\) is used strictly to compute local speeds of sound \(a = \sqrt{\gamma \bar{P}/\rho}\) at the left and right states. This prevents imaginary sound speeds or overly slow waves from collapsing the Riemann fan in expansion regions.

Rule 2: The regularized pressure \(P_{reg}\) must be used to compute total enthalpy, energy-associated star states, and pressure-flux splittings. Using \(P_{phys}\) here violates the underlying hyperbolic bulk-viscosity momentum transfer, generating massive spurious oscillations at shocks.

2. Complete HLLC Formulation with PPR

The HLLC Riemann solver defines three wave speeds: the left wave (\(S_L\)), the right wave (\(S_R\)), and the intermediate contact wave (\(S_*\)). Because PPR adds a 6th variable (\(S = \rho P_{phan}\)), we must determine its behavior in the star states (\(\mathbf{U}_{*L}\) and \(\mathbf{U}_{*R}\)).

Since the phantom pressure state is a passive scalar advecting strictly with the fluid velocity, it travels at the contact wave speed \(S_*\). Therefore, the 6th variable's star-state value is computed dynamically by scaling the advected scalar fraction by the star-state density.

Acoustic Wave Speed Bounds

The outer wave speeds \(S_L\) and \(S_R\) are computed using Einfeldt's pressure-velocity bounds based on safe acoustic properties:

$$a_L = \sqrt{\gamma \frac{\max(P_{reg,L}, P_{phys,L})}{\rho_L}}, \quad a_R = \sqrt{\gamma \frac{\max(P_{reg,R}, P_{phys,R})}{\rho_R}}$$
$$S_L = \min(q_L - a_L, \, \tilde{q} - \tilde{a}), \quad S_R = \max(q_R + a_R, \, \tilde{q} + \tilde{a})$$

where \(q = \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{n}\), and \(\tilde{q}, \tilde{a}\) represent the standard Roe-averaged velocity and speed of sound.

The Contact Wave Speed (\(S_*\))

The contact wave speed \(S_*\) must use the regularized pressure to ensure momentum conservation across the bulk-viscous shock:

$$S_* = \frac{P_{reg,R} - P_{reg,L} + \rho_L q_L (S_L - q_L) - \rho_R q_R (S_R - q_R)}{\rho_L (S_L - q_L) - \rho_R (S_R - q_R)}$$

The 6D Star State Vector (\(\mathbf{U}_{*K}\))

For \(K = L\) or \(R\), the intermediate state \(\mathbf{U}_{*K}\) is written as:

$$\mathbf{U}_{*K} = \rho_K \left(\frac{S_K - q_K}{S_K - S_*}\right) \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ u_K + (S_* - q_K)n_x \\ v_K + (S_* - q_K)n_y \\ w_K + (S_* - q_K)n_z \\ \frac{E_K}{\rho_K} + (S_* - q_K)\left( S_* + \frac{P_{reg,K}}{\rho_K (S_K - q_K)} \right) \\ \frac{S_K}{\rho_K} \end{bmatrix}$$

Notice that the 6th element is simply the advected phantom scalar fraction \(\frac{S_K}{\rho_K}\) multiplied by the intermediate star density \(\rho_{*K}\).

3. Complete AUSM+ Formulation with PPR

The Advection Upstream Splitting Method (AUSM+) separates the convective and pressure components of the flux vector.

To accommodate PPR, we redefine the normal flux splitting on the interface as:

$$\mathbf{F}^{AUSM} = \dot{m}_{1/2} \mathbf{\Psi}_{1/2} + \mathbf{P}_{1/2}$$

Convective and Pressure Vectors with Regularized Enthalpy

The convective transport vector \(\mathbf{\Psi}\) must utilize the **regularized total enthalpy** \(H_{reg}\) to ensure regularized energy dissipation across shocks:

$$H_{reg} = \frac{E + P_{reg}}{\rho}$$
$$\mathbf{\Psi} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & u & v & w & H_{reg} & \frac{S}{\rho} \end{bmatrix}^T$$

The pressure vector \(\mathbf{P}\) is populated strictly by the regularized pressure \(P_{reg}\):

$$\mathbf{P} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & P_{reg}n_x & P_{reg}n_y & P_{reg}n_z & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}^T$$

Interface Mass Flux (\(\dot{m}_{1/2}\)) using Safe Speed of Sound

The interface Mach number is evaluated using the average local speed of sound \(a_{1/2}\), computed using the safe pressure bounds to prevent non-physical wave speeds:

$$a_L = \sqrt{\gamma \frac{\max(P_{reg,L}, P_{phys,L})}{\rho_L}}, \quad a_R = \sqrt{\gamma \frac{\max(P_{reg,R}, P_{phys,R})}{\rho_R}}$$
$$a_{1/2} = \frac{1}{2}(a_L + a_R), \quad M_L = \frac{\mathbf{u}_L \cdot \mathbf{n}}{a_{1/2}}, \quad M_R = \frac{\mathbf{u}_R \cdot \mathbf{n}}{a_{1/2}}$$

The split Mach number \(M_{1/2}\) and pressure \(P_{1/2}\) are computed using standard AUSM+ polynomial definitions:

$$M_{1/2} = \mathcal{M}^+(M_L) + \mathcal{M}^-(M_R)$$ $$P_{1/2} = \mathcal{P}^+(M_L)P_{reg,L} + \mathcal{P}^-(M_R)P_{reg,R}$$

Finally, the interface mass flux is defined as:

$$\dot{m}_{1/2} = a_{1/2} M_{1/2} \begin{cases} \rho_L & \text{if } M_{1/2} \ge 0 \\ \rho_R & \text{if } M_{1/2} < 0 \end{cases}$$
